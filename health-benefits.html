Health Benefits of using standing desks

Would you wonder about workplace and home office, changing to adapt using standing desks, from traditional furnitures? If it is the case, this topic is yours to help you full understanding of standing desks.

Before Standing desks

The Dangers of Prolonged Sitting

Sitting for extended periods can be harmful to your health, causing various health problems, including obesity, cardiovascular disease, and type 2 diabetes. And Can result back and neck pain.

This is a major problem for office workers, as they sit down for most of the day. Fortunately, standing desks are becoming increasingly more popular.

Why use Standing Desks?

Here are 7 benefits related to using standing desks for various aspects of health and productivity:

Burn Calories and Keep Fitness

Just by standing and moving around a little will help burn calories, thereby maintaining fitness. Studies suggest that standing at a desk can burn up to 50-110 extra calories per hour compared to sitting.

Alleviate Back and Neck Pain

Alternate sitting and standing to ease back and neck strain. Improve posture.

Tips for a Good Posture
  • Stand with your feet hip-width apart, flat feet on the floor and relax your shoulders
  • Keep your head straight
  • Keep your arms loosely at your sides, with your 90 degrees bent elbows
  • Take regular breaks to move around, stretch, and relax your back, neck, and shoulders

Improve Your Posture

Standing desks promote better spinal alignment, reducing the likelihood of poor posture. Encourages an open and upright stance, positively impacting overall body alignment.

Keep Your Circulation Going

Mixing it up during the day helps to keep your blood circulation going, which leads to sharper brain function.

Increase productivity

Varying your stance can help to lift your mood and energy levels. The simple act of movement and variation has huge mental health benefits and enhances productivity.

Lower Blood Sugar Levels

Study involving 23 office workers found that alternating between standing and sitting every 30 minutes throughout the workday reduced blood sugar spikes by 11.1% on average. And by spending more time standing at work, your risk of high blood sugar and type 2 diabetes is significantly reduced.

Reduce risk of sickness

The sedentary lifestyle of traditional office work can lead to more sickness. But with standing desks, they can reduce the risk and lose fewer days to sick leave.

Conclusion

   Incorporating standing desks into your routine not only addresses immediate health concerns but cultivates lasting well-being. By promoting physical activity, reducing sedentary habits, and fostering an ergonomic workspace, standing desks emerge as a holistic solution for enhancing fitness, mitigating pain, refining posture, sustaining circulation, boosting productivity, managing blood sugar levels, and fortifying your resilience against illness. Embrace the positive impact of standing desks on your overall health, creating a pathway to a more active, comfortable, and productive lifestyle.
